Angiography

An imaging technique that uses X-rays and contrast dye to visualize blood vessels.

Annular Size

The measurement of the valve annulus to ensure proper fitting of a prosthetic valve.

Annulus

The ring-like structure that supports and holds a heart valve in place.

Anticoagulation Therapy

Treatment aimed at reducing blood clot formation.

Aortic Regurgitation

A condition where the aortic valve does not close properly, allowing blood to flow backward.

Aortic Root

The section of the aorta closest to the heart where the aortic valve is located.

Aortic Root Enlargement

Expansion of the root often associated with medical intervention or underlying illness.

Aortic Stenosis

Narrowing of the aortic valve opening, restricting blood flow from the heart.

Aortic Valve

The valve between the left ventricle and the aorta that regulates blood flow out of the heart.

Atrial Fibrillation

An irregular and often rapid heartbeat originating from the atria.

Balloon Aortic Valvuloplasty (BAV)

A procedure to widen a narrowed aortic valve using a balloon catheter.

Balloon-Expandable Valve

A valve that is expanded and positioned using a balloon catheter during implantation.

Balloon-Tipped Catheter

A catheter with a balloon at its tip used for various diagnostic and therapeutic procedures.

Bioprosthetic Valve

A heart valve derived from animal tissue or other biological materials.

Cardiac Catheterization

A diagnostic procedure where a catheter is inserted into the heart to evaluate and treat conditions.

Cardiac Network

The network of specialized cells that regulates the heart’s electrical impulses.

Cardiac Output

The amount of blood the heart pumps per minute.

Cardiac Rehab

A program of exercise and education for patients after heart surgery or illness.

Cardiopulmonary Bypass

A technique where a machine temporarily takes over the heart and lungs during surgery.

Cardiothoracic Surgeon

A surgeon specializing in the heart and chest.

Cardiovascular Surgery

Surgery related to the heart and blood vessels.

Conduction Abnormality

Irregularities in the heart’s electrical conduction system.
